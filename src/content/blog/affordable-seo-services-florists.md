## Why Florists Need SEO

When someone needs flowers for a birthday, anniversary, funeral, or wedding, they search Google. "Florist near me" and "flower delivery [city]" are among the most common local searches, and the flower shops that rank at the top capture the orders. Meanwhile, florists who depend on marketplace sites like FTD or 1-800-Flowers hand over a massive cut of every sale to the middleman.

SEO puts your flower shop directly in front of customers who are ready to order, without the marketplace commissions eating into your margins. Your website, your prices, your profit.

## How Customers Find Florists Online

Most flower customers start with a Google search, especially for time-sensitive occasions. The queries that drive the most orders include:

**High-intent searches florists should target:**
- "florist near me"
- "flower delivery [city name]"
- "wedding florist [city name]"
- "funeral flowers [city name]"
- "same day flower delivery [city name]"
- "roses delivery near me"
- "Valentine's Day flowers [city name]"

These are customers ready to place an order right now. Ranking for them means capturing sales that would otherwise go to online marketplaces or competitors.

## What Affordable Florist SEO Includes

### Google Business Profile Optimization

Your Google Business Profile is where customers check your reviews, hours, and photos before placing an order. We optimize your categories, delivery areas, product photos, and description so you stand out in the local map pack. A strong review strategy turns happy customers into your best marketing asset.

### Occasion and Product Pages

Different occasions drive different searches. Wedding flowers, sympathy arrangements, birthday bouquets, corporate event florals, and holiday specials each need their own page. A customer searching for "sympathy flowers" has completely different needs than someone planning a wedding centerpiece, and your website should serve both.

### Same-Day and Delivery Pages

"Same day flower delivery" is one of the highest-converting searches in the floral industry. A dedicated page targeting this term — with clear ordering deadlines and delivery areas — captures customers who need flowers fast and are willing to pay for convenience.

### Location and Delivery Area Pages

If you deliver to surrounding cities and neighborhoods, each delivery area needs its own optimized page. A florist in downtown Portland that delivers to Beaverton, Lake Oswego, and Tigard should rank in all of those communities.

### Seasonal Content

Flower demand spikes around Valentine's Day, Mother's Day, prom season, and the holidays. Content published ahead of these seasons — gift guides, arrangement ideas, ordering deadlines — captures early searchers and positions your shop as the go-to choice when the rush hits.

## Common SEO Mistakes Florists Make

Many flower shops leave orders on the table because of avoidable mistakes:

- **Depending on marketplace sites** — FTD, Teleflora, and 1-800-Flowers take 20-30% of each order; your own website keeps that revenue
- **No product photos** — Stock photos of generic arrangements don't build trust; real photos of your work convert browsers into buyers
- **Missing delivery information** — If customers can't quickly find your delivery area and same-day cutoff time, they'll order from someone who makes it clear
- **No occasion-specific pages** — A generic "Shop Flowers" page can't compete with competitors who have dedicated wedding, sympathy, and holiday pages
- **Ignoring seasonal keywords** — Failing to publish Valentine's Day or Mother's Day content months before the holiday means missing the early planners
